# Legal and Claims Boundaries

This page defines how to present SynapticFour Showcase content in a legally careful, non-overclaiming way.

## Scope of this repository

- This repository provides documentation, orchestration guidance, and example workflows.
- It does not provide legal advice, regulatory certification, or medical advice.
- It is intended for technical evaluation and customer discovery planning.

## Allowed claim style

Use statements such as:

- "can support"
- "can help teams"
- "is designed to"
- "evidence-oriented"
- "readiness-focused"

Avoid absolute or legally risky statements such as:

- "is compliant/certified"
- "guarantees compliance"
- "automatically satisfies EHDS or other regulations"
- "secure by default in all environments"

## Compliance and regulatory framing

- Outputs from Ferrum/HELIOS/related tooling are technical evidence artifacts.
- Such artifacts may support audit and compliance workflows.
- Formal compliance determinations remain with the customer organization and its legal/compliance functions.

## Security and privacy framing

- Security and privacy outcomes depend on customer configuration, infrastructure, access controls, and operational discipline.
- Showcase examples are not a substitute for security architecture review, threat modeling, penetration testing, or data protection impact assessments where required.

## AI/RAG framing

- RAG and assistant outputs must be treated as assistive and reviewable.
- Human review and source verification remain required, especially for regulated decisions.
- Data ingestion boundaries and role-based access controls must be explicitly defined by the deploying organization.

## Evidence and timeline framing

- Time estimates in this repository are indicative planning guides, not contractual commitments.
- Proof phases should define explicit acceptance criteria, assumptions, and decision gates.

## Commercial and legal communication checklist

Before sharing externally, verify that materials:

- use conditional language ("can", "may", "designed to"),
- avoid certification/compliance guarantees,
- separate technical evidence from legal conclusions,
- identify assumptions and customer responsibilities,
- include this page in customer-facing documentation paths.
